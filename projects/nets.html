Climbing with lights
Watch Video

Climbing with nets is the outcome of the final project from the Physical Interaction course at KTH University. The main objective was to design and construct an interactive prototype meant for use in dark Swedish winter playgrounds.

Concept Design

For our project, we decided to further develop a typical climbing net. To get inspiration, we carried out a bodystorming session, visiting different playgrounds around KTH University.

Once we had explored different designs and implementations of the nets, we brainstormed about ways to improve these climbing structures during dark and cold environments. One idea that stood throughout the session was the use of lights. Since we also wanted to invite creativity and playfulness, we finally decided to add the music twist to it.

In the end, we concluded that we would build a climbing net with the following characteristics:

  • It would have a semi-sphere shape to bring inclination to the net and avoid possible falls.
  • When no user is interacting with the nets, the prototype will light up to invite users.
  • When a user starts climbing the net, each section of the net that is pulled will light up and trigger a sound.
  • Each column of the prototype will represent an instrument or collection of sounds.
  • When two or more sections of the net are pulled, the sounds will mix together.
