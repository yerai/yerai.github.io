Flowlight
Watch Video View on Github

Flowlight was the outcome of a small material exploration project from the Physical Interaction course at KTH University. The main objective of the exploration was to obtain material knowledge specific to light and interaction and learn about constructionism.

During this four days project, we explored and experimented with physical controls and technology related to interactive lightning. In my case, a pair of NeoPixel LEDs.

The project was divided into three phases:
First, we focused on connecting the NeoPixel LEDs to the Arduino Uno to try out different light modes, colors, and brightnesses.

Once we had explored the given technology, we came up with different ideas to interact with the lights. At the end of this process, we decided to go with two ultrasonic sensors: one for the different modes at the top and other for the color selection on the side.

Lastly, we designed the structure and built it using cardboard, white paper, and hot glue.
