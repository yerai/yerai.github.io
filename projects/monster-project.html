The Monster Project

This render was the result of a Character and Set Design course at the University of Bristol. The main goal of the project was to experience the entire static character production pipeline from concept and design, through geometry creation, lighting design, texturing and shader application.

Design

The approach for the character design was to create a monster-like character inspired by real children's drawings of monsters. For my design, I utilized an illustration from the 2011 Hanham Abbot School Gallery.

Original Drawing
First Draft

As portrayed in the first sketch, I decided to go for a three-stages monster with long hair, big eyes, pointy mouth, and skirt. After more iterations of this first draft, I searched for inspiration and created a small reference board. I also thought about different possible set designs but ended up going for the lipstick rocket with the moon and space on the background.

Modeling

For this character, I started from the feet to the head and subsequently modeled the set. Most of the model is polygonal cubes with extruded and smoothed faces.

Other elements of the monster were made extruding faces over a curve and later translating selected vertex to achieve the desired shape. The body, on the other hand, was modeled using three connected spheres.

To make the braids I modeled three polygons that, after being stretched into the desired length were twisted around their central pivot using a twist animation deformer.

Lastly, I modeled the set, starting with the lipstick rocket. This was made out of multiple cylinders and square polygons. The moon, on the other hand, was changed to have a heart shape out of a cube.

Texturing

The shaders used on the design are either blinn or phong, depending on the reflection of the material. The following illustration presents all the shaders applied.

Regarding the textures, I used displacement maps to give 3D character and authenticity to the model. To achieve this, images collected from the internet are projected over the mesh.

Lighting design

For the lighting design of my final render, I used two kinds of lights: directional and ambient.

Directional lights are used to give the impression the monster is being illuminated by the sun or star in the space. Ambient lights were placed near the character and the moon to contrast with the dark space background.
