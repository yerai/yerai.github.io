Mobile Ticket Application

This mobile application design was the result of a User Experience course at Universidad Politecnica de Madrid. The main goal of the project was to design and develop a prototype that would solve a problem new Madrid residents encounter.

We composed and carried out a quick questionnaire to people that had recently moved to the city. According to our results, many participants had problems with the public transportation system.

Right after this, we carried out another questionnaire focusing on a single target group: newly arrived students. Some of the key aspects discovered were that:

  • Card readers don't always work properly when entering the station.
  • There's no way to see if the ticket has expired before trying to enter the station.
  • The process to obtain a new card is a hassle.
  • Users consider annoying using multiple apps.

From the information gathered, we discerned two different user profiles:

  • Regular users: own a monthly pass and use public transportation daily.
  • Occasional users: purchase single or ten trip tickets and are not used to public transportation.

In order to identify the main pain points and moments of truth of our users, we created a user journey map.

We then designed and built the high-fidelity prototype using Justinmind. Because of the course briefness, we skipped the layouts and low-fidelity stages of the process.

Lastly, we created two different task scenarios to test our prototype with real users.

Purchase a ticket

Your current physical metro card has expired. You need to buy new tickets, and want to test the new ticket feature of the app.

  • Buy a 10-trip ticket for the Metro in zones A-B2.
  • You have already set up Google Pay on your phone, which you always like to use.
Find your route

You are at university. You have just been called by the company for your internship and they want to have an interview with you next Monday. The company is at Plaza de España and you need to find out at what time you can be there.

  • Next Monday is the 28nd of January.
  • Your classes end at 17.00 next Monday.
  • Find the fastest connection to Plaza de España for the interview.
