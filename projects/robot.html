Beat of my Drum
Watch Video

This animated short was the result of an Animation Production course at the University of Bristol. The main objective of the project was to choreograph a set of simple base shapes to elegantly come together building a complex humanoid robotic character that will start to dance.

Pre-production and planning

During the first few weeks, I spent a significant amount of time researching, designing and storyboarding the shot before animating. The movement and choreography of the build were also taken into account when designing the geometry.

In the end, I came up with a design inspired on a Star Wars character with some modifications to fit the project requirements.

Pit Droid from Star Wars
Final Character Design

With this in mind, I created a short storyboard to guide me during the animation process.

Regarding the dancing moves, I got great inspiration from two sources: First, making the character create the word "LOVE" using its arms from the "YMCA" video by Village people. Secondly, a free-style section with fast movements similar to the "La Macarena" dance.

Implementation

The robot is made out of 56 different parts but because of its symmetry, some parts were mirrored to form the final result.

To move the pieces of the robot around, I used keyframes, setting both the first and last frames with the attribute editor. In many cases, I relied on the squash and stretch animation principle to avoid rigidity. Regarding the camera movements, I employed a perspective camera with many smoothed transitions utilizing the graph editor.

Making the robot dance was done utilizing joints and handles. I divided the model into different sections but since I wanted to restrict its movements to resemble a robot I didn't apply many joints.

Texturing and Lightning

For the textures, I divided the elements of the robot in two groups and applied different types of rusty metal images to the shapes.

Regarding the lightning, I applied two types: ambient light in the middle of the set and a directional light to properly illuminate the robot.
