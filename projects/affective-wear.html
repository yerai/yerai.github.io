Affective wear

This project came to life thanks to the Interactive Media Lab and Ph.D. Associate Professor Maki Sugimoto, who supervised me during my Erasmus + program at Keio University, Japan.

The main goal behind the project was designing and developing a cross-platform application service to classify, store and display facial expressions recorded by a wearable device.

This device, developed by the Interactive Media Lab, records the distance between the userâ€™s face and the wearable. With this information, the application uses machine learning to classify the data into one of the eight possible facial expressions that can be recorded and stores it in a database.

Users can then access the application to visualize their recorded facial expressions in two ways: using a time-based localization, providing users with statistics, or using a location-based visualization, providing users with an interactive map.

The design and development of this application service had two main goals in mind: allow users to have a better understanding of how emotional states vary in their daily life and discern places where they had good or bad times according to their mood.

The whole application was developed using Unity. Besides, many Google Cloud Services and APIs such as Maps Geocoding and Cloud Prediction were actively used.
