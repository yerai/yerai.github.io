Movie Interactive Visual Analysis Tool
Demo View on Github

The goal of this project was the design and implementation of a new interactive tool related to the movie industry. We thought that it would be interesting to identify the different factors that make a movie successful, not only in terms of popularity but also in terms of profitability.

We observed that people pay special attention to the movies that are either better-ranked or have won an award. Diverse factors such as the number of votes, trends, and budgets are important for profitability and popularity. However, they don't always guarantee how highly a movie will be rated. With this project, we sought to understand these factors and find new hypotheses about them.

The dataset used contains information from IMDb (Internet Movie Database) from 1893 till 2005. With this information and the visualization tool three main questions are addressed:

  • Which genres of movies are most prevalent among the years?
  • How has movie budget affected rating?
  • How has the number of votes and distribution of average movie rating changed over time?
Regarding the implementation three main technologies were used: Shiny, CSS, and Plot.ly.
